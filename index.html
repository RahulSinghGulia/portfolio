Research & Thesis

Indoor Wireless Communication for UAVs and IoT

Thesis work on accurate indoor path loss modeling for UAV, IoT, and cloud robotics. Proposed the UAV Low Altitude Air to Ground (U-LAAG) statistical path loss model, adapted from ITU-R, for 2.4–2.5 GHz ISM band. Validated via experiments in university premises, dwellings, and basements. Compared with ITU-R, Two-ray, and Log-distance models; U-LAAG closely matches measured results, enabling robust indoor network planning for UAVs and IoT deployments.

MAC Layer Contention Window Project (CSMA/CA Simulation)

Python-based discrete-event simulation of CSMA/CA using simpy. Quantitatively analyzed the effect of RTS/CTS, network size, exponential backoff, and coverage area on throughput, collisions, latency, and channel efficiency. Features include: carrier sensing, DIFS/SIFS, random backoff, ACK handling, collision detection, and comprehensive performance visualization using matplotlib.

Selected Projects

Evo-WISVA: Dynamic Channel Digital Twin for Smart Warehouses

Developed AI/ML digital twin framework predicting wireless channel conditions in dynamic warehouse environments achieving MAE=0.329 dB. Secured industry funding from Toyota Raymond Corporation. Average prediction speed: 0.000496 seconds per frame.

Stabilized Symbolic Regression for Interpretable BLER Modeling

Physics-informed symbolic regression framework discovering analytical expressions for V2X BLER. Used -ln(BLER) target transformation and aggressive genetic programming (20k population, 300 generations) to achieve R²=0.86 and MSE=2.08×10⁻² on unseen data.

Interpretable Classification of Respiratory Patterns via Wi-Fi CSI

Developed feature-engineered pipeline using Analytic Signal Method (Hilbert Transform) on filtered CSI data. Created transparent geometric model achieving accuracy comparable to complex LSTM systems while ensuring full interpretability.

MD-OFDM: Energy-Efficient & Low-PAPR MIMO-OFDM System

Achieved 5 dB PAPR reduction and 60% reduction in RF chain power consumption in simulations. Directly addresses PHY-layer energy efficiency challenges.

About Me

I hold a Ph.D. in Wireless Communication and work at the intersection of machine learning, mmWave wireless systems, and digital twin modeling. My research focuses on enabling deterministic, low-latency, and adaptive wireless networks for autonomous and industrial environments.

Research Highlights

1. A Memory-Augmented Generative Framework for Spatio-Temporal mmWave Channel Prediction in Dynamic Industrial Environments

This Ph.D. research introduces Evo-WISVA, a memory-augmented generative AI framework for real-time spatio-temporal mmWave channel prediction in dynamic industrial environments. The model enables self-evolving wireless digital twins capable of proactive, low-latency network adaptation.

  • High Accuracy: Achieves 1.75 dB MAE, outperforming state-of-the-art benchmarks.
  • Robustness: Effectively adapts to unseen environments and limited training data.
  • Efficiency: Sub-millisecond per-frame inference suitable for real-time deployment.

Real-World Impact: Enables autonomous, self-optimizing wireless networks for URLLC and Industry 4.0 by overcoming the computational and adaptability limits of traditional ray-tracing and simulation-based planning tools.

2. CSMA/CA Contention Window Simulation (MAC Layer)

Python-based discrete-event simulation of the IEEE 802.11 CSMA/CA protocol using SimPy, enabling analysis of contention behavior, RTS/CTS mechanisms, and exponential backoff strategies.

3. UAV Low-Altitude Air-to-Ground (U-LAAG) Path Loss Model

A statistical indoor path loss model for low-altitude UAV and IoT communications, validated through extensive real-world measurements and shown to outperform traditional ITU-R and log-distance models.
